what i learnt:
AI reflects back what you give it

The quality of your output directly mirrors the quality and specificity of your input. Vague prompts like "generate a summary of what I learnt in this" give thin results — detailed context gives detailed analysis.

Prompting is a learnable craft

You can direct AI like a director — specify tone, format, aesthetic, audience, and structure all at once. The more intentional your framing, the more cinematic the output.

AI can't read what isn't there

For data-driven tasks (GitHub summary, analytics, reports), you must supply the raw data. AI can format and synthesise — it cannot invent facts. Always bring the inputs.

Self-reflection has leverage

Using AI to analyse your own patterns — how you work, think, and communicate — is one of the highest-ROI uses. It externalises your blind spots and turns them into an action plan.

Iteration > perfection in one shot

Asking for everything at once (personality profile + portrait + GitHub summary) in a single prompt is ambitious but leaves gaps. Breaking it into steps produces more precise results each time.
