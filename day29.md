## Features of the Simulator

- Dynamic supply chain crisis scenarios
- Three strategic crisis response decisions
- Supplier negotiation simulation
- CEO leadership decision rounds
- AI investment recommendations
- Executive business dashboard
- Crisis performance score

---



## Key Learnings

### What is Supply Chain Crisis Management?

Supply chain crisis management is the process of identifying, responding to, and recovering from disruptions that affect procurement, manufacturing, logistics, and customer delivery while minimizing business impact.

---

### Simulation Workflow

1. Review company profile
2. Analyze the assigned crisis
3. Select three response strategies
4. Negotiate with suppliers
5. Answer CEO leadership questions
6. Invest in AI supply chain solutions
7. Review executive dashboard
8. Analyze final crisis score

---

## Major Decision Areas

### Crisis Response

Choose actions that balance operational continuity, customer satisfaction, and financial performance.

### Supplier Negotiation

Negotiate delivery timelines, pricing, and long-term partnerships while maintaining supplier relationships.

### Leadership Decisions

Respond to executive-level challenges involving stakeholders, customers, employees, and investors.

### AI Investments

Evaluate technologies that improve forecasting, procurement, inventory optimization, and supply chain resilience.

---

## Executive Dashboard

The Executive Dashboard summarizes key business metrics, including:

- Operational Efficiency
- Customer Satisfaction
- Inventory Stability
- Financial Performance
- Supplier Relationship Health
- Business Continuity
- Overall Crisis Score

---

## One New Thing I Learned

I learned that effective supply chain management is not only about logistics—it requires balancing customer expectations, supplier collaboration, financial constraints, and operational resilience. AI-powered forecasting and procurement tools can significantly improve decision-making and help organizations respond faster to unexpected disruptions.
