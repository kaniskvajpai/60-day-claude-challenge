# Key Learnings

- Investors evaluate much more than the product—they assess the founder's execution capability.
- A strong competitive moat is often built through proprietary data, customer relationships, and workflow integration rather than features alone.
- Investors expect a detailed execution roadmap for the first 90 days after funding.
- Scalability must come from automation and software rather than increasing manpower.
- A clear understanding of burn rate, runway, and profitability timelines significantly improves investment readiness.
- Positioning NutriScope AI as a complement to laboratory testing, rather than a replacement, strengthens the value proposition.
- Enterprise customers prioritize measurable ROI, consistency, and operational efficiency.
