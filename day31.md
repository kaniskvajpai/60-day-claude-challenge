## Key Learnings

### What is a Supply Chain Control Tower?

A Supply Chain Control Tower is a centralized monitoring system that provides real-time visibility into logistics, suppliers, inventory, transportation, and customer operations. It enables organizations to detect disruptions early and make faster, data-driven decisions.

---

## Simulation Workflow

1. Review the operational dashboard.
2. Monitor incoming supply chain alerts.
3. Prioritize incidents based on urgency and business impact.
4. Select the most effective corrective actions.
5. Observe KPI changes after each decision.
6. Handle multiple alerts simultaneously.
7. Review the final operational performance dashboard.
8. Replay with different scenarios to improve performance.

## Major Operational Challenges

### Incident Prioritization

Determine which disruptions require immediate action while balancing limited operational resources.

### Corrective Actions

Choose responses that minimize business impact and restore normal operations as quickly as possible.

### KPI Management

Every operational decision directly influences financial performance, customer experience, inventory levels, and service quality.

## One New Thing I Learned

I learned that a Supply Chain Control Tower is much more than a monitoring dashboard—it acts as the central decision-making hub for enterprise operations. By combining real-time data with AI-driven insights, organizations can quickly prioritize incidents, coordinate responses across teams, and improve overall operational resilience while maintaining customer satisfaction.
