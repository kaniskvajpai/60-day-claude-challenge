# Day 21 – Digital Privacy Dashboard

## 🔐 Overview

For **Day 21** of the **#60DayClaudeChallenge**, I used Claude to generate a complete **Digital Privacy Dashboard** as a standalone HTML application.

The dashboard analyzes a sample digital footprint dataset and visualizes privacy risks through interactive charts, scores, rankings, and simulations. It provides actionable insights into online exposure and demonstrates how AI can transform raw privacy data into meaningful visual analytics.

---

# 🎯 Objective

Build an interactive dashboard that helps users:

* Measure their Digital Footprint Score
* Analyze their Privacy Score
* Identify data exposure risks
* Visualize exposure across companies
* Understand their Digital Twin Profile
* Simulate privacy improvements
* Receive an overall privacy verdict

---

# ▶️ How to Run

1. Clone or download the repository.
2. Open `digital-privacy-dashboard.html` in any modern web browser.
3. Explore each dashboard section.
4. Analyze the provided sample dataset.
5. Interact with the Privacy Improvement Simulator.
6. Review the final privacy assessment.

---

# 🧪 Dashboard Sections Explored

## 1. Digital Footprint Score

Displays an overall estimate of the user's online presence based on the sample dataset.

---

## 2. Privacy Score

Evaluates the user's current privacy posture and highlights areas requiring improvement.

---

## 3. Exposure Heatmap

Visualizes which categories of personal information have the highest exposure levels.

---

## 4. Company Exposure Ranking

Ranks organizations based on the amount of user data they potentially possess.

---

## 5. Digital Twin Profile

Provides a profile summarizing how an organization or advertiser might infer user characteristics from available digital data.

---

## 6. Risk Radar

Illustrates multiple privacy dimensions including:

* Identity Risk
* Tracking Risk
* Financial Risk
* Social Exposure
* Device Exposure
* Data Sharing Risk

---

## 7. Most Valuable Data Assets

Highlights the types of personal information that are most valuable to advertisers and data brokers, such as:

* Email Address
* Phone Number
* Location History
* Browsing Activity
* Purchase History
* Device Information

---

## 8. Privacy Improvement Simulator

Demonstrates how adopting stronger privacy practices (such as limiting permissions, enabling MFA, or reducing data sharing) can improve the overall privacy score.

---

## 9. Final Verdict

Summarizes the user's overall digital privacy status and recommends practical steps for improving online security.

# 🔍 Key Privacy Findings

* Multiple online services contribute to an expanding digital footprint.
* Data sharing practices directly affect overall privacy exposure.
* Strong authentication and permission management significantly reduce risk.
* Exposure heatmaps help identify the most vulnerable data categories.
* Privacy scores improve when unnecessary data sharing is minimized.
* Interactive visualizations make complex privacy concepts easier to understand.
