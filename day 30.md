## Key Learnings

### What is Supply Chain Optimization?

Supply Chain Optimization is the continuous process of improving sourcing, manufacturing, warehousing, transportation, and inventory decisions to maximize efficiency while balancing cost, speed, customer satisfaction, and operational resilience.

---

---

## Business Decision Areas

### Supplier Strategy

Evaluate supplier diversification, cost, quality, and reliability to reduce procurement risks.

### Factory Location

Choose manufacturing locations that balance production costs, labor availability, market proximity, and logistics efficiency.

### Warehouse Strategy

Optimize storage capacity, regional distribution, fulfillment speed, and operational costs.

### Transportation

Compare air, road, rail, and sea transportation based on delivery speed, cost, flexibility, and environmental impact.

### Inventory Management

Balance inventory levels to minimize stockouts while avoiding excessive carrying costs.

## One New Thing I Learned

One important learning is that supply chain optimization is about making continuous improvements instead of rebuilding systems from scratch. Every decision—from supplier selection to transportation and inventory management—creates trade-offs between cost, speed, risk, sustainability, and customer satisfaction. Small improvements across multiple areas can significantly enhance overall business performance.
